finEQUITY

UI/UX Study Case

I started this project as part of my UI/UX bootcamp where we sought to do a redesign of a non-profit website. The first steps were to research and speak to stakeholders about what their goal for finEQUITY was.

Project Overview

Research: Conducted user research to understand the goal of the company and the target audience. In addition, gathered feedback from stakeholders to identify areas for improvement in the existing site.

Goals & Objectives: Identifying the new vision and current pain points, in order to improve the user experience and increase awareness.

Design & Development: Developing wireframes of the new site design with a focus on mobile.

Testing: Conducted testing to ensure the new designs were functional and resolved pain points.

Problem Statement

Families and individuals affected by incarceration often don’t have foundational building blocks in place for financial success, such as checking accounts, healthy credit scores, credit cards, and retirement savings. Without the foundational background, individuals are more prone to recidivism and repeating the cycle rather than breaking it. FinEQUITY aims to reduce these barriers and introduce users to these concepts through correspondence courses, micro-loans and online instruction—however, finEQUITY currently doesn’t have the digital infrastructure to achieve this.

Solution

We created a responsive mobile design of the site that properly executes their shift in focus to a more user friendly site. We prototyped a cleaner, more streamlined website that draws attention to the services offered.
